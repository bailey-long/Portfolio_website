Top Secret

Old School RuneScape

Runescape is a game I have been playing off and on for years. I like the game so much due to how free it is, there is no goal except to better yourself and achieve your own personal goals. It's often seen as the game where you spend hundreds of hours doing the same thing and while not completely untrue, there is an incredible amount of content meaning you could play this game for an absurd number of years before running out of things to do.

Magic the Gathering

I started playing this incredible (and expensive) card game in high school. Magic is a card game about multidimensional conflict, cataclysmic threats to the multiverse, and the struggle of the planeswalkers to keep said multiverse from falling apart. The variation of play styles is what makes this game so fun, and the fact that you can play it with friends or strangers.

Dungeons & Dragons

Where do I begin with D&D, most people know it as "That board game from Stanger Things", D&D is a game with no limits, a game where you can be a powerful wizard fighing off even more powerful monsters, a lighthearted bard that lives for the nightlife or a simple baker that makes a living from a small shop in the capital city. I have been playing D&D since I was 14 and have met my closest friends through it.
